# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two segments through one sequenced funnel (see PRD.md): travelers with a China/Taiwan trip weeks-to-months out who need practical conversational Chinese fast, and the same people post-trip converting into long-term learners. Desktop-oriented web in v1; the heavy learning happens at a desk during trip prep. They arrive motivated but intimidated by characters and tones; the job is "make me able to handle real situations, and make it feel doable."

## Product Purpose

MoZhi (默知) gets a beginner using the official 2025 HSK 3.0 Level 1 and Level 2 Mandarin syllabus in real situations quickly: 300 Level 1 words and 200 additional Level 2 words. Learners can choose one Hanzi, build a custom set, or open real-life sets such as shopping and small talk. Hanzi progress separates form and meaning, sound, and contextual use before calling a word proficient. Short, authored role calls accept Hanzi, pinyin, or recognized speech; optional AI wording feedback confirms the intended meaning first and offers one useful correction without pretending to hear pronunciation details that are not available. Success means completing a useful communicative goal in the current session. No lesson, section, level, set, or test depends on a streak, spaced-retention gate, or earlier completion.

When a local Ollama model is available, role-call feedback stays on-device by default. Anthropic remains an optional cloud provider, and every provider failure returns to deterministic authored feedback without interrupting the conversation.

## Brand Personality

Playful, warm, encouraging. A friendly robot study-buddy, not a stern teacher and not a corporate platform. Bold color and big type used with confidence; celebratory, never childish. The hanzi themselves are the visual heroes: the brand treats Chinese characters as beautiful, decodable design objects.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: cream/purple gradient heroes, identical feature-card grids, stock illustration people.
- Corporate edtech (Coursera, LinkedIn Learning): sterile blue professionalism, certificate-mill tone.
- Avoid orientalist shorthand even when reaching for "Chinese-ness": no brush-stroke kitsch; let real hanzi typography do the work.

## Design Principles

1. **The characters are the art.** Hanzi at display scale, treated typographically, beat any illustration or stock asset.
2. **Conversation first.** Every surface should pull toward talking with Xiao Hui; the robot is a character, not a decoration.
3. **Confidence over cuteness.** Playful through scale, motion, and color commitment, not through emoji and rounded-everything.
4. **Make progress feel inevitable.** Encouraging copy and visible momentum; never guilt, never red-pen energy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for state transitions and feedback. Pinyin and English remain available as learner-controlled scaffolds. Every task is keyboard navigable, voice has a text fallback, and progress is never communicated by color alone.
